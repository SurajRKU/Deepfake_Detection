Deepfake Detection

Deepfakes are images or videos that are synthetically manipulated or generated using deep learning. The term deepfake to refer to both media manipulation and media generation through deep learning. It is sometimes used for “face swaps,” “expression or attribute manipulation,” and even for images entirely synthesized by a deep learning algorithm.Though it’s possible for humans to detect deepfakes, it’s getting harder as the general quality increases. Moreover, as it gets easier and faster to create high quality deepfakes, the volume of deepfake content may be too great for human detection alone. That’s why we may need to rely on algorithms to determine whether a piece of content is a deepfake or not. Fortunately, deepfake detection research is ongoing, and offers numerous potential benefits. Not only are algorithms automatic, they can also potentially detect cues that are hard for humans to find on their own. Onevariant of the deep networks is deep autoencoders, which have been widely applied for dimensionality reduction and image compression.

In this work, we have implemented image comparison metrics and neural architectures that use convolutions to detect features that can be used to classify videos into fake or real.This is done in a sequence of steps. First, frames from the video are extracted. Then, these frames are passes as an input to the face extraction module. The face extraction module crops faces from the frames. These cropped faces are then passed to the architecture which applies the knowledge it has learned from the training proces and classifies the video into real or fake. The quality of the detection can be improved by training the model for more epochs and fine tuning the process for higher resolution. In our future work we look to train our model on samples from multiple datasets. The datasets available are CelebDF, Face2Face, FaceForensics. We would also want to explore and compare metrics such as intradataset performance and cross dataset performance.
